extra-hands brand reference · v0.1

Direction

The extra-hands identity is built around quiet competence. This is a tool that gets things done without ceremony — calm, warm, and reliably present. The aesthetic sits between a craftsperson's workshop and a well-worn desk: analog warmth expressed through clean geometry.

The mark is three parallel rounded fingers — a minimal abstraction of an open, helping hand. Not a robot arm, not a sparkle, not an arrow. A hand. Human, direct, unpretentious. The middle finger stands tallest, left and right frame it; together they suggest reach, readiness, and the act of handling work on someone's behalf.

The warm brown accent carries through from mark to wordmark hyphen, tying the identity together without needing a brand color splashed everywhere. The typography (Playfair Display) gives the wordmark editorial weight — this is a considered tool, not a startup commodity.
